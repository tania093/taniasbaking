Hazelnut Chocolate Cake Recipe

Ingredients

  • 7-ounce package skinned and roasted hazelnuts, (1 3/4 cups), divided
  • 2 cups granulated sugar
  • 3 whole eggs
  • 1 1/2 teaspoons vanilla extract
  • 1 1/2 teaspoons ground cinnamon
  • 1 teaspoon espresso powder, optional
  • 1 cup all-purpose flour
  • 1 cup butter (2 sticks)
  • 4 ounces Baker’s unsweetened chocolate

Frosting

  • 1 1/2 cups semisweet chocolate chips
  • 1/2 cup heavy cream
  • 1 tablespoon butter
  • 1/2 teaspoon espresso powder, optional
  • 3/4 cup Nutella
  • 1/2 cup confectioners’ sugar

Instructions

  1. Preheat oven to 350 degrees F.

  2. Prepare a 9×9-inch pan by lining with two sheets of folded aluminum foil so that foil handles stick up on all four sides. Spray the inside with kitchen pan spray.

  3. Place 1¼ cups roasted peeled hazelnuts in a food processor and pulse a few times to break them up. Add granulated sugar and process for about ten seconds or until the consistency of beach sand.

  4. Add eggs and vanilla and pulse a few times to mix.

  5. Add ground cinnamon, espresso powder, and flour, and pulse to combine.

  6. In a large microwave-safe bowl, place butter and Baker’s chocolate and microwave in three 30-second bursts, stirring each time.

  7. Remove from microwave and keep stirring until all the chocolate is dissolved.

  8. Add the mixture from the food processor and stir until completely mixed.

  9. Pour into the prepared pan and bake for 35 minutes or until a toothpick inserted into the center comes out clean. Check at the 30-minute mark.

  10. Let cool for 5 minutes in the pan then lift the foil handles and transfer to a cooling rack and cool completely.

  11. When ready to frost, place semisweet chocolate chips and heavy cream in a medium microwave-safe bowl and microwave for 30 seconds and stir. Add butter and espresso powder and microwave for 30 more seconds.

  12. Stir mixture until smooth and then add the Nutella and confectioners’ sugar and stir to a creamy thick consistency.

  13. Spread over the top of the cooled brownies.

  14. Take the remaining ½ cup of hazelnuts and place between a folded sheet of parchment paper and smash a few times with the bottom of a heavy pan to crush, leaving some larger pieces.

  15. Sprinkle this over the frosted brownies and cut into 9 (3×3) or 12 (3×4) portions and serve.
